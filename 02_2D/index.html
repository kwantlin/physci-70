Week 2: 2D Design & Cutting


This week, we learned to do both basic circuit construction and 2D cutting for a press-fit construction kit. Both these topics were new to me as a CS student, and I enjoyed beginning to get hands-on with the process. Here's what I made!

First, we completed an in-class activity surrounding building a circuit both virtually and with a microcontroller. I utilized the Arduino "Blink" sketch as the base, incorporating an LED and a resistor as well as adding wiring to a breadboard.

Blink Sketch

We also worked in groups to create a press-fit construction kit using Fusion 360. I worked with Haeyun Lee and Joy Lin. Joy had experience with solidworks and came up with an ambitious idea for a pirate ship. After she constructed the body of the ship, I took note of the dimensions and created the modified hull piece below. Haeyun was able to pick up the pieces on campus and construct them.

From there, I was able to attach an anchor. Creating something that would be press-fit was challenging to visualize mentally, but after considering some possibilities, I decided to make a "nail" that the anchor could "hook onto". This isn't the normal way an anchor would attach, but the beauty of 3D modeling is imagination. :) This video introduced me to cutting holes both through the "Hole" construction and through extrusion cuts.


This video helped with my first assemblies, as I was able to create "joints" between faces to align components precisely.
